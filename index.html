Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was a chairman of the Tata Group from 1990 to 2012, and interim chairman from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts.

ABOUT TATA GROUP

The Tata Group is one of India's oldest and largest conglomerates, with its origins dating back to 1868. Founded by Jamsetji Tata, the group has grown into a global enterprise with interests spanning multiple sectors including steel, automotive, information technology, telecommunications, hospitality, and more. Here are some key points about the Tata Group: Diversified Business Portfolio: The Tata Group operates in various industries such as Tata Steel, Tata Motors, Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), Tata Power, Tata Chemicals, Tata Communications, Tata Consumer Products, Tata Realty, Tata Capital, and Tata Sons, among others. This diversification helps the group to balance risks and leverage opportunities across different sectors. Global Presence: The Tata Group has a significant global footprint with operations in over 100 countries across six continents. It has manufacturing and distribution facilities, research and development centers, and sales offices worldwide. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR): The Tata Group has a long-standing tradition of corporate social responsibility. The Tata Trusts, which own a significant portion of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, are involved in various philanthropic activities including healthcare, education, rural development, and poverty alleviation. Innovation and Technology: Several Tata companies are leaders in their respective industries and are known for their innovation and adoption of cutting-edge technologies. For instance, Tata Motors is a major player in the automotive industry and has been investing in electric and alternative fuel vehicles. TCS is one of the largest IT services firms globally and is known for its expertise in digital technologies. Leadership and Governance: The Tata Group has been led by visionary leaders such as Ratan Tata and Cyrus Mistry. Leadership transitions in the group are carefully managed, and the Tata Sons board oversees the strategic direction of the conglomerate. Acquisitions and Partnerships: Over the years, the Tata Group has pursued strategic acquisitions and partnerships to expand its business reach and capabilities. Notable acquisitions include Tata Steel's acquisition of Corus Group, Tata Motors' acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover, and Tata Global Beverages' partnership with Starbucks. Sustainability Initiatives: The Tata Group is committed to sustainability and environmental conservation. Many Tata companies have implemented sustainable practices and initiatives aimed at reducing their carbon footprint and promoting eco-friendly business operations. Overall, the Tata Group is a prominent player in the global business landscape, known for its ethical business practices, commitment to excellence, and contributions to society.
